Exploratory Data Analysis of Conflict in Myanmar
- UCDP data

I conducted an exploratory analysis of conflict-related deaths in
Myanmar using data from the Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP).

Project Overview: This project focused on uncovering patterns of violence, particularly civilian casualties, in key events such as the 2017 Rohingya genocide and the 2021 military coup. Additionally, I evaluated both UCDP and Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED) datasets and chose UCDP for its restrictive, well-defined criteria, making it more suitable for analysing broad conflict trends.

Data Analysis Process:
• Qualitative Research: To frame the data analysis, I first conducted qualitative research into Myanmar’s conflict history. This research helped contextualise the data and allowed me to interpret it with a deeper understanding of the country's political and ethnic tensions.
• Data Cleaning & Exploration: I used Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Bokeh) to clean and analyse the UCDP dataset, identifying key trends in conflict-related deaths over time.
• Visualizations: Created time series charts, bar graphs, and geographic visualizations to highlight spikes in deaths, particularly during the Rohingya genocide and post-coup violence.

Key Findings:
• 2017 Rohingya Genocide: A significant spike in civilian deaths was observed, aligning with reports of widespread ethnic violence.
• Post-2021 Coup: Ongoing violence has led to a rise in deaths, with civilians consistently being the most affected group.
• Civilian Casualties: Across almost all conflicts in Myanmar, civilians accounted for the majority of deaths.

Data Source Evaluation
Through a review of the datasets, I determined that UCDP’s strict definitions allowed for a more focused measure of large-scale conflicts compared to ACLED, which captures smaller, localised events but was less suitable for analysing long-term trends.

Impact
This project highlights the devastating impact of Myanmar's conflicts on civilian populations and provides a detailed look at the patterns of violence across key historical events. These insights are valuable for policymakers, researchers, and humanitarian organisations looking to better understand and respond to ongoing violence in Myanmar.
